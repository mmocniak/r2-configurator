How this model works — assumptions & method

What's modeled month by month

The engine runs an actual amortization schedule, then walks every month of your ownership horizon adding up cash out: financing (down payment day one, then each monthly payment until the loan is paid), insurance, electricity, registration + EV fee, yearly property tax on the depreciating value, and maintenance. Gear & accessories and charger install hit as day-one cash. The 2025–28 loan-interest deduction is credited in the years it applies, and resale value is recovered at the very end — that final number is your true cost.

The value curve

Depreciation is modeled as a smooth decay anchored to your resale-retained %: value lands exactly on that percentage at the end of your horizon, so the Depreciation & underwater chart and the resale credit always agree. The crossover is where the car's value rises above the loan balance — the month you stop being underwater.

Taxes, fees & the deduction

Upfront tax applies to price + destination − trade (vehicle only — gear isn't taxed); the rate and the title / registration / annual EV-surcharge fees vary by state — they load from your state's defaults, and you can edit any of them to match your county. Deduction phases out $200 per $1,000 of MAGI over $100k single / $200k joint, gone at $150k / $250k; value = deductible interest × your marginal rate. Not tax or financial advice.
